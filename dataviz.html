Timeline: While Otranto is largely considered to be the first Gothic novel, it was not the first novel to have those motifs. The popularity of the genre erupted in the 1790s. Gothic literary scholar Nick Groom suggests that the demand for more "horrid" stories had something to do with the unsettling stories of the French Revolution.

Motifs: Looking at the Key Players' motif graph, I am struck by how much they don't have in common. More than have of the motifs only occur once among the four novels, which points to their relative originality.

Publishers: William Lane and A.K. Newman were prolific publishers of the Gothic, joining together to form the "Minerva Press." Interestingly, our key players were not originally published by those specializing in the genre (later editions are another story). See also the maps page.

Trends in Motifs: With this graph, I am trying to illustrate the popularity of motifs over time. The list of motifs are arranged in order of descending frequency, while patches of blue demonstrate multiple books being published at the time with the same motifs. As you scroll down the list of motifs, they get more... unique and less frequent. The second version shows where our key players fall in this arrangement - it looks more like a player piano scroll.
